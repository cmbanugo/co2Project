    Carbon Footprint

    General Information

    What is a carbon footprint

    A carbon footprint is the amount of carbon dioxide and other carbon compounds emitted due to the consumption of fossil fuels by a particular person or group. The term footprint refers to the impact that we have on the Earth. By adding the word carbon to the beginning we get the phrase carbon footprint which is the amount of carbon dioxide we are creating.

    Reducing Your Carbon Footprint

    Ways to reduce my carbon footprint

    Diet

    Eat locally produced and organic food

    • 13% of US Greenhouse gas emissions results from the production and transportation of food

    Cut out beef and dairy

    • It takes a lot of resources to rause cows
    • Methane is produced from the animals' bodily waste and methan is another destructive greenhouse gas.
    • Animal agriculture is responsible for 18% of Greenhouse Gas emissions, more than the combined exhaust from all transportation
    • Livestock and their byproducts are responsible for at least 32 billion tons of carbon dioxide per year or 51% of all world wide greenhouse emissions
    • Emissions for agriculture projects are supposed to increase by 80% by 2050
    • Cows produce 150 billion gallons of methane per day

    Compost your food waste

    Vehicle Travel

  • Try to walk/ride your bike more often that you drive. If you have to drive try to carpool.
  • Drive a low carbon vehicle
  • electric cars emit no CO2 if they are charged with clean electricity.
  • Properly inflated tires can improve your mileage by up to 3%
  • Avoid traffic by using traffic apps

    • Air Travel

  • Take fewer and longer vacations that are farther away
  • Fly less frequently

    • Fly economy class because each flyer splits the value of the carbon emissions

    Home Energy

    Appliances

  • make energy efficiency a top priority in your home by buying appliances with the ENERGY STAR label

    • Lighting

    replace incandescent lightbuls with compact fluorescents and or LED bulbs.

    Thermostat

    Refrain from using heater or A/C when not necessary and turn these systems off when you leave your home

    Solar

    • Add solar panels to the roof of your home
    • Two companies have reduced upfront costs for this usually pricy option.

    Participate in low-energy activities. Instead of sky diving go running.

    Minimize the amount of purchases you make. Refrain from indulging yourself with products too often.

    Water Usage

    Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle

    29% if US Greenhouse Gas emissions is from the provision of good (excludes food)
